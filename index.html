About Sightsavers

Sightsavers India has been working in more than 100 rural districts and 20 cities across India since 1966, to eliminate avoidable blindness and promote equality of opportunity to people with visual impairment. They have educated, counselled, trained and rehabilitated people who are visually impaired or blind, and helped extend the reach of eye services to the least served areas of India. They have also been working in the field of inclusive education, eye health, and disability in India.

Who is a Visionary?

A visionary is someone who has:
  • The power to change someone’s life
  • A heart to uplift the less fortunate
  • The vision of a future free from avoidable blindness

What does a visionary support?

The proceeds from the Visionaries are channelled into these programs of Sightsavers India:

Netra Vasant

Creating awareness, providing quality eye health services & eliminating avoidable blindness among the rural population.

Amrita Drishti

Creating comprehensive & sustainable eye health mechanisms in the urban slums of India

Vidyajyoti

Screening children in government schools to identify and prevent eye diseases and vision impairments among children.

Raahi

Providing eye-health services to the overworked truckers community for ensuring road safety.

Child inclusion program

Helping the visually impaired and disabled children cope-up with the normal children.

Social inclusion program

Training the disabled and visually impaired individuals to gain confidence, build livelihoods and support their family.
